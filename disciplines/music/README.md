## 13 Notes: Why Not 12?

Western music uses a 12-tone scale. But the framework's natural number is R = 13. Why 12?

Because the 13th "note" is the octave — the return to the starting pitch one octave higher. The 13 points of PG(2,3) map to:

- 12 distinct pitch classes within one octave
- 1 return point (the octave, which is identified with the starting point)

The 12-tone equal temperament scale is the projection of the 13-point projective plane onto the 12-step circular pitch space. The "missing" 13th note is the octave identification — the topological closure that makes the pitch space circular rather than linear.

This is why the circle of fifths has 12 notes and closes exactly: it is the boundary of the PG(2,3) point set projected onto the pitch axis.
